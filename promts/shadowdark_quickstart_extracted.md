# Shadowdark RPG: Player Quickstart Guide

## Introduction

Shadowdark RPG is a fantasy adventure game where you and your companions delve into buried ruins, lost cities, spider-infested forests, and even fearsome dragon lairs in search of gold and glory.

The Shadowdark is any place where danger and darkness hold sway. It clutches ancient secrets and dusty treasures in its rotting claws, daring fortune seekers to tempt their fates.

With your adventuring companions at your side, you confront the Shadowdark's sinister traps, formidable magics, and ruthless monsters. If you survive, you'll bring back untold riches plucked from the jaws of death itself. And before long, you'll hear the Shadowdark's call once again!

### What Defines This Game?
Speed, danger, and simplicity. Magic is perilous, and battles are fast and deadly. Being clever is crucial for survival. Shadowdark RPG is rules-light and intuitive. It encourages quick decisions rather than hunting through the rulebook for answers.

In this game, a torch only holds back the pressing darkness for one hour of real-world time. There isn’t a moment to waste when the flames are burning low...

## The Basics

If this is your first time playing Shadowdark RPG, it’s helpful to start with a premade character. These quickstart rules cover beginner characters for their first few adventures.

### Characters
You decide your character’s actions and tell them to the Game Master (GM). The GM determines if your actions are successful and describes the results. Always ask yourself: If you were really your character, what would you do right now?

### Describe an Action
On your turn, describe an action you want to do. For example, you could say you’re going to shoot your bow at a troll. The GM might ask you to make a check to determine whether your action succeeds.

### Movement
During your turn, you can move up to **near** (roughly 30 feet). You can break this movement up in any way. If you don’t take an action, you can move **near** again.

### Rounds
Each person takes a turn, starting with the GM and going clockwise. Once everyone has taken a turn, that completes one round.

### The Dice
You'll need d4, d6, d8, d10, d12, and d20 dice.
*   **Multiples:** 3d6 means roll three six-sided dice and add them.
*   **Advantage:** Roll two times and use the higher result.
*   **Disadvantage:** Roll two times and use the lower result.

### Stats
Stats range from 3-18 and have a modifier (MOD) from -4 to +4.
*   **Strength:** Physical power.
*   **Dexterity:** Agility, reflexes.
*   **Constitution:** Endurance, resistance to injury.
*   **Intelligence:** Logical ability.
*   **Wisdom:** Instinct, willpower.
*   **Charisma:** Appeal, presence.

### Checks
Roll a d20 and add a modifier. If the total equals or beats the Difficulty Class (DC), you succeed.
*   **Easy:** DC 9
*   **Normal:** DC 12
*   **Hard:** DC 15
*   **Extreme:** DC 18

### Gear Slots
Carry a number of items equal to your **STR stat** or **10** (whichever is higher). Most items take one slot. Bulky items may take more.

### Light Sources
*   **Torch:** Sheds light to **near** distance. Burns for 1 hour of real time.
*   **Lantern:** Casts light to **double near** distance. Requires oil. 1 flask = 1 hour.
*   **Campfire:** Combines three torches. Lasts 8 hours. Casts light to **near**.

## Character Creation

### Overview
1.  **Name:** Choose a name.
2.  **Ancestry:** Your heritage (Dwarf, Elf, Goblin, Halfling, Half-Orc, Human).
3.  **Class:** Your job (Fighter, Priest, Thief, Wizard).
4.  **Stats:** Roll 3d6 in order for each stat (Strength, Dexterity, Constitution, Intelligence, Wisdom, Charisma).
5.  **Background:** Your history and past experience.
6.  **Alignment:** Lawful, Neutral, or Chaotic.
7.  **Deity:** A cosmic power you worship.
8.  **HP:** Hit Points (injury capacity).
9.  **AC:** Armor Class (how hard you are to hit). 10 + DEX mod + Armor.
10. **Gear:** Buy with 2d6 x 5 gp.

### Ancestries
*   **Dwarf:** Stout. +2 HP. Roll HP per level with advantage. Languages: Common, Dwarvish.
*   **Elf:** Farsight. +1 to ranged attacks or spellcasting checks. Languages: Common, Elvish, Sylvan.
*   **Goblin:** Keen Senses. Can't be surprised. Languages: Common, Goblin.
*   **Halfling:** Stealthy. Once per day, invisible for 3 rounds. Languages: Common.
*   **Half-Orc:** Mighty. +1 to melee attack and damage. Languages: Common, Orcish.
*   **Human:** Ambitious. One additional talent roll at 1st level. Languages: Common + one extra.

### Classes

#### Fighter
*   **Weapons:** All weapons.
*   **Armor:** All armor and shields.
*   **Hit Points:** 1d8 per level.
*   **Weapon Mastery:** +1 to attack/damage with one weapon type. Add half level (round down) to rolls.
*   **Grit:** Choose STR or DEX. Advantage on checks of that type to overcome force.
*   **Hauler:** Add positive CON mod to gear slots.

#### Priest
*   **Weapons:** Club, crossbow, dagger, mace, longsword, staff, warhammer.
*   **Armor:** All armor and shields.
*   **Hit Points:** 1d6 per level.
*   **Spellcasting:** Cast priest spells. Uses Wisdom.
*   **Turn Undead:** Can force undead to flee or destroy them.

#### Thief
*   **Weapons:** Club, crossbow, dagger, shortbow, shortsword.
*   **Armor:** Leather, mithral chainmail.
*   **Hit Points:** 1d4 per level.
*   **Backstab:** Extra weapon die damage on unaware targets. +1 die per 2 levels.
*   **Thievery:** Advantage on Climbing, Sneaking, Hiding, Disguises, Traps, Picking Pockets, Opening Locks.

#### Wizard
*   **Weapons:** Dagger, staff.
*   **Armor:** None.
*   **Hit Points:** 1d4 per level.
*   **Spellcasting:** Cast wizard spells. Uses Intelligence.
*   **Learning Spells:** Can learn from scrolls (DC 15 INT check).

## Gameplay Mechanics

### Time
*   **Real Time:** 1 hour in reality = 1 hour in-game (used for torches/lanterns).
*   **Turns:** Each player acts in order.
*   **Rounds:** Completed when everyone has taken a turn.

### Movement Distances
*   **Close:** 5 feet.
*   **Near:** 30 feet.
*   **Far:** Within sight.

### Resting
*   **Rest:** 8 hours sleep + 1 ration.
*   **Benefit:** Regain all HP and stat damage.
*   **Interruption:** DC 12 CON check if combat occurs. Failure means no benefit.

### Stealth and Surprise
*   **Hiding/Sneaking:** DEX check vs searcher's WIS check.
*   **Surprise:** One free turn before initiative is rolled. Advantage on attacks against surprised targets.

### Combat
1.  **Initiative:** Roll d20 + DEX mod. Highest goes first, then clockwise.
2.  **Turn Action:** One action + move **near**.
3.  **Attacks:** d20 + MOD vs AC. Critical hit on natural 20 (double damage).
4.  **Dying:** 0 HP = unconscious and dying. Death timer = 1d4 + CON rounds. Natural 20 on turn = rise with 1 HP.

### Spellcasting
*   **Wizard:** 1d20 + INT mod. DC = 10 + Tier. Fail = Lose spell until rest + Mishap on natural 1.
*   **Priest:** 1d20 + WIS mod. DC = 10 + Tier. Fail = Lose spell until rest. Natural 1 = Deity displeased (requires Penance).
*   **Focus Spells:** Maintain with a check at the start of each turn. Cannot cast other focus spells while focusing.
